Italian Cuisine (Italy)

Dish: Pasta Carbonara, Carbonara is a pasta dish made with eggs, hard cheese, cured pork, and black pepper1234. The cheese is usually Pecorino Romano, parmesan, or a combination of the two1. The dish took its modern form and name in the middle of the 20th century1. The pasta is usually spaghetti or another long pasta4. The dish is made by cooking bacon, eggs, and parmesan mixed into hot cooked spaghetti3. The hot pasta partially cooks the eggs and creates a silky carbonara sauce34.

Japanese Cuisine (Japan)

Dish: Sushi (various types), Japanese cuisine is the traditional cooking of Japan, also known as washoku, that emphasizes variety and balance of ingredients, flavors, colors, and techniques12. It is influenced by seafood, seasonal and regional dishes, and fermentation134. Japanese cuisine can be light or hearty, and includes staples like rice, noodles, soup, pickles, and okazu side dishes15. Japanese cuisine also has many types of restaurants, from mobile stands to theme places3.

Indian Cuisine (India)
Chicken Tikka Masala is a popular and flavorful dish that originated in Indian cuisine but has become a favorite in many parts of the world. It typically consists of succulent pieces of marinated and grilled chicken (chicken tikka) served in a rich, creamy, and mildly spiced tomato-based curry sauce (masala). The chicken is first marinated in a mixture of yogurt and spices, including garam masala, cumin, coriander, and turmeric, which imparts a vibrant color and robust flavor.

Mexican Cuisine (Mexico)

Dish: Tacos al Pastor, Tacos al Pastor is a popular Mexican street food dish that originated in central Mexico. The name "al Pastor" means "in the style of the shepherd" in Spanish. This dish is influenced by the traditional shawarma or gyros cooking method brought by Lebanese immigrants to Mexico. The dish typically features thin slices of marinated pork that are stacked on a vertical rotisserie, similar to the method used for shawarma

Chinese Cuisine (China)

Dish: Peking Duck, Peking Duck is a crispy, aromatic duck dish known for its thin, crispy skin and succulent meat. The preparation typically involves air-drying the duck before roasting it until the skin becomes crispy and the meat remains tender. The duck is often served with thin pancakes, hoisin sauce, and sliced scallions. To enjoy it, diners typically spread hoisin sauce on a pancake, add slices of duck and scallions, then roll it up like a burrito.

French Cuisine (France)

Dish: Coq au Vin, Coq au Vin is a classic French dish that translates to "Rooster in Wine." This hearty and flavorful dish is a slow-cooked stew made with chicken (often substituted for rooster in modern recipes) braised in red wine, typically Burgundy, along with mushrooms, onions, carrots, and bacon. The dish is seasoned with garlic, thyme, and bay leaves, which infuse the chicken with a rich and savory flavor.

Thai Cuisine (Thailand)

Dish: Pad Thai,Pad Thai is a popular Thai stir-fried noodle dish that has gained international acclaim for its delicious flavors and versatility. The dish typically features flat rice noodles stir-fried with a combination of ingredients such as tofu or shrimp, bean sprouts, peanuts, egg, and lime. The flavors are balanced with a sweet and savory sauce made from tamarind paste, fish sauce, and other seasonings. Garnishes often include fresh cilantro and chopped peanuts, adding a delightful crunch and freshness to the dish.

Spanish Cuisine (Spain)

Dish: Paella, Paella is a traditional Spanish dish originating from the Valencia region. It is a flavorful and aromatic one-pan rice dish known for its vibrant colors and rich combination of ingredients. The key components of paella typically include rice, saffron for its distinctive yellow color and unique flavor, a variety of proteins such as chicken, rabbit, seafood, or a mix of these, along with vegetables like tomatoes, bell peppers, and green beans. The dish is often seasoned with paprika and garlic, giving it a robust taste.

Greek Cuisine (Greece)

Dish: 'Moussaka', Moussaka is a traditional Greek dish that is made with layers of eggplant, minced meat (often lamb or beef), and potatoes, all topped with a rich béchamel sauce. The dish is typically baked until golden brown, creating a hearty and flavorful casserole. The layers of eggplant and potato are often fried before assembling the dish, adding a crispy texture to the soft and savory interior. Moussaka is seasoned with a variety of herbs and spices, such as cinnamon and oregano, giving it a distinctive Mediterranean flavor.

Brazilian Cuisine (Brazil)

Dish: Feijoada, Feijoada is a traditional Brazilian stew that is considered the national dish of Brazil. It originated in the 19th century and has Afro-Brazilian roots. The dish is known for its rich and hearty flavors, combining black beans with various cuts of pork, such as sausage, bacon, and pork ribs. Some variations may include beef or other meats.

Moroccan Cuisine (Morocco)

Dish: Tagine, Tagine is a traditional Moroccan dish named after the special earthenware pot in which it is cooked. The dish itself is a slow-cooked stew that typically combines meat (such as lamb, chicken, or beef) with a variety of vegetables, fruits, and aromatic spices. Common ingredients include onions, tomatoes, garlic, olives, preserved lemons, and a blend of spices like cumin, coriander, cinnamon, and saffron.

Korean Cuisine (South Korea)

Dish: Bibimbap, Bibimbap is a popular Korean dish that translates to "mixed rice" in English. It is a colorful and flavorful bowl consisting of warm white rice topped with an array of seasoned vegetables, often including sautéed spinach, carrots, bean sprouts, and shiitake mushrooms. Additionally, it is typically adorned with a fried egg, sliced beef (or another protein), and a dollop of gochujang (spicy red chili paste). The dish is known for its vibrant presentation and the harmonious blending of different tastes and textures.
